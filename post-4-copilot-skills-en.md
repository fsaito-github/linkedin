🧠 Copilot Skills: Teaching AI Your Team's Expertise

Plugins connect Copilot to external tools. But what if you want to teach Copilot how your team works — your coding standards, architectural patterns, and domain-specific knowledge?

That's where Copilot Skills come in. They let you encode your team's expertise into reusable capabilities that Copilot can apply during conversations and code generation.

𝗦𝗸𝗶𝗹𝗹𝘀 𝘃𝘀. 𝗣𝗹𝘂𝗴𝗶𝗻𝘀: 𝗪𝗵𝗮𝘁'𝘀 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗗𝗶𝗳𝗳𝗲𝗿𝗲𝗻𝗰𝗲?

→ Plugins connect to external systems (APIs, databases, third-party tools)
→ Skills encode knowledge and behavior (coding patterns, review criteria, domain rules)

Think of plugins as Copilot's hands (they let it interact with tools) and skills as its training (they shape how it thinks and responds).

𝗪𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗖𝗮𝗻 𝗬𝗼𝘂 𝗕𝘂𝗶𝗹𝗱?

→ Code Review Skill — Define your team's review checklist. Copilot applies it automatically: security patterns, error handling, naming conventions.
→ Architecture Skill — Teach Copilot your microservices patterns. When a dev asks "How should I structure this new service?", Copilot responds with your team's conventions — not generic advice.
→ Domain Skill — Working in fintech? Healthcare? Encode compliance requirements and domain terminology so Copilot speaks your language.

𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗦𝘁𝗿𝗮𝘁𝗲𝗴𝗶𝗰 𝗩𝗮𝗹𝘂𝗲

Skills are how you scale your best engineers' knowledge across the entire team. Instead of documenting standards that nobody reads, you embed them directly into the AI tool everyone already uses.

That's not just productivity — it's institutional knowledge preservation.
